No one saw her come <br>
No one saw her leave <br>
She had been cursed to the shadows  <br>
At least that's what people believe  <br>

But shadows are kind  <br>
And darkness a great teacher  <br>
They didn't let her feel alone  <br>
They became her preacher  <br>

They taught her all they had seen  <br>
From robbery to murder  <br>
They made sure she wouldn't starve  <br>
Into their comrade they had turned her  <br>

And perhaps everything would have been fine  <br>
If she wouldn't have turned too greedy  <br>
For things always tumble down  <br>
When one becomes all needy  <br>

She tried to rob the necklace <br>
Off the minister's wife <br>
It's not that big of a crime, the shadows protested <br>
But they still imprisoned her for life <br>

Maybe it was for the better <br>
Now she's guaranteed productive work to do <br>
And two square meals a day <br>
A life without worry of rapists <br>
And a shower to clean away the grease <br>

It's not as rosy as it sounds <br>
For she would have to lead a life confined <br>
But isn't that the fate of all beautiful birds <br>
To be locked away in a cage and for food made to grind <br>

But anyhow who would have employed her outside <br>
For she is unkind and unkempt <br>
She doesn't have any manners, remember <br>
She has been cursed to the shadows with no exempt <br>

>Sometimes I sit back and wonder <br>
>Did a skilled girl like her <br>
>Purposefully commit a crime so naive <br>
>In an effort to change the course <br>
>Of her woefully guilty life <br>

>Now she can sleep peacefully <br>
>Knowing that my wallet and yours are finally safe from her <br>
